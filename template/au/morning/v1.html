Guardian Australia's Morning Mail
Tuesday 01 Jul 2014
g
Subsection 1

 

The Refugee Council of Australia has warned that Australia would "flagrantly" violate the refugee convention if 153 Tamil asylum seekers were sent to Sri Lanka without being allowed to lodge a claim for protection.

There have been unconfirmed reports that those on the boat, which was reportedly stranded near Christmas Island at the weekend, have been handed over to the Sri Lankan navy.
 
Meanwhile, the ousted Papua New Guinea assistant police commissioner has claimed that the main suspect in Reza Barati's death, a former Salvation Army worker, has fled to the PNG mainland. 
 
And an asylum seeker who lost an eye in the violence in which Barati was killed is suing the government and security firm G4S, claiming they failed in their duty of care.
